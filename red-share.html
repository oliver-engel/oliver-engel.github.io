Red Share is an augmented social media experience for today's political climate.


The viewer logs into their Facebook account–on the official Facebook website–and finds that the content and structure of their newsfeed has become warped. Names of friends and family are swapped out or translated to Russian, external links lead to fake news stories and propaganda, and elements on the page are jumbled and visually intrusive. Some of the content is left intact, as they would normally see it. The comfort that they expect in the social media environment is absent, replaced with violent distortions of truth and form.

The work exists as both a Google Chrome Extension and an installation.


Red Share is a Google Chrome Extension, written in Javascript with the help of the News API and P5.js. It works by identifying text in Facebook posts, and swapping it out for content that's sure to be divisive: alt-right news headlines and anonymous reddit comments are some of the sources. Some of the original text is left intact, creating a disorienting mix of strange propoganda mixed with posts from friends and family.

In its installation form, Red Share is presented on an old CRT TV hooked up to a computer; there's a horrible high-pitched whine from the tubes, and everything on the desk has an 80s nostalgia to it. Mundane objects are painted black, giving the desk an ominous aesthetic that focuses attention onto the monitor.
