Osler, The Asthma Management App

How we disfigured the design double-diamond and still made it out okay

Osler is a care management tool for parents of kids with asthma. It links with the child's electronic health record to power an individualized care experience for the most common chronic childhood disease.

About

Premera Health, a medical insurance company, presented us with an area they were interested in exploring: improving the caregiver experience. There’s a lot at play here: the complexities of medical insurance, the many players involved in medical care, and the fact that we’re designing for an industry that many (including myself) view as fundamentally broken. This was also my first time using the “double diamond” design method–naturally, I made a ton of mistakes, which I’ll do my best to point out.

Setting the stage: who's a caregiver?

Rapid ideation expanded our notion of who a caregiver might be: maybe they were the elderly caring for their spouses, adult children supporting their parents, or parents with young kids at home.

We became particularly interested in the parent as a caregiver to a child–and after a fair amount of literature reviews, forum-crawling, and informal Facebook polls, formed a few assumptions:

  • Parents caring for children with chronic diseases have to constantly manage the child's condition and find the right care.
  • Parents often have trouble finding the right pediatrician due to a mix of accessibility, child-rearing techniques, and cost.

Armed with our assumptions, we set out to the Seattle Children’s Museum and talked to 7 families, polled 280+ parents on Facebook, and did an additional 2 user interviews.

Long story short: we were wrong. Nothing much interesting came out of our probing. We were asking the wrong questions.
