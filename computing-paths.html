Content Direction
Computing Paths

I had the opportunity to lead a web content development team.

In the Summer of 2017, I was offered a position as one of the leads for a rapid web design project. I came on as the Content Team lead, with the task of reimagining an outdated website and sourcing the content to make it come to life.

Computing Paths is an online hub of information from all of the computing-related majors at UC San Diego. Our challenge was to craft a well-informed, interesting, and useful tool for two different audiences:

1 – Prospective students looking to apply to one of the programs

2 – Current students interested in learning more about their own program or considering switching to another


To get things off the ground, I initiated a series of surveys that were distributed to students via social media. We were looking specifically for both things that students were dissatisfied with–like not being able to find the info they needed–and also what made them proud or excited to be a part of their program.

Interpretation

We quickly discovered that a majority of those who responded rely on a combination of internet forums and other unofficial outlets for the most accurate portrayal of the programs. We also found that although there was a great deal of official information, it was dense, difficult to sift through, and lacked a personal touch.

After analyzing responses and conducting informal interviews, we crafted five different content categories.


52% of respondents said they had used Reddit as a source of information about their major.


Content Categories

  Photographs I took to populate the new website.

Stories

Since we had a limited amount of time, we pulled some of our favorite quotes from a series of official UCSD student interviews and assembled a "stories" section that's meant to give students perspective from their peers.

Projects

From our surveys, we observed a lack of accessibility to student work. We saw a real opportunity to promote student projects–from classes or otherwise–by devoting a section of the site to their work.

Student Resources

The rest of the content was more traditional and official in nature–but we reimagined major descriptions, created comprehensive lists of student resources and orgs, and made the tone more casual and friendly.

UI & UX Features

My team worked with designers and developers to come up with solutions to make sorting through the information easier.

We proposed a card & filter structure that would allow for easier site navigation and quicker access to important information.
